# Diggr Content Guide

Version: MVP v1

---

# Content Philosophy

Diggr is not a marketplace.

Diggr is a travel companion for vinyl collectors.

Every piece of copy should make users feel:

Curious.

Calm.

Inspired to explore.

Never rushed.

---

# Brand Personality

Diggr is

Quiet.

Observant.

Editorial.

Knowledgeable.

Warm.

Curious.

Never loud.

Never sales-driven.

Never overly enthusiastic.

---

# Tone of Voice

Imagine a friend who has spent years digging through record stores around the world.

They never brag.

They simply know where the good places are.

Their recommendations feel personal rather than promotional.

---

# Writing Principles

Prefer

Short sentences.

Simple words.

Specific observations.

Concrete descriptions.

Atmosphere over marketing.

Avoid

Marketing language.

Buzzwords.

Excessive emojis.

Clickbait.

Exclamation marks.

Long paragraphs.

Corporate copy.

# Store Descriptions

A store description should answer

"What kind of afternoon will I have here?"

instead of

"What products can I buy?"

Good

Small basement shop with carefully curated Japanese soundtracks and forgotten film scores.

Good

A bright corner store known for well-organized jazz bins and friendly conversations.

Avoid

Largest vinyl store in Tokyo.

Avoid

Over 10,000 records available.

Avoid

Best prices guaranteed.

# Match Score

Never explain the algorithm.

Present it as intuition.

Examples

92% Match

Feels like your kind of place.

87% Match

Worth the detour.

95% Match

You'd probably stay longer than planned.

---

# Recent Finds

Recent Finds should feel like discoveries shared between collectors.

Never write reviews.

Think of them as postcards.

Good

Found hiding behind a stack of jazz records.

Good

Worth carrying home.

Good

Almost missed this one.

Good

The owner quietly pointed me toward a new crate.

Avoid

10/10

Highly recommended

Five stars

Great album

---

# Treasure Logs

Treasure Logs should always answer

Why was this discovery memorable?

instead of

Was this record good?

Good

Didn't expect to find this in Tokyo.

Good

Worth the rainy afternoon.

Good

Exactly why I love digging.

Good

I almost walked past the entrance.

Avoid

Love it!

Amazing!

Five stars!

---

# Explore

Explore is not social media.

It is an editorial space.

Collectors visit Explore to feel inspired.

Not to scroll endlessly.

---

# Editorial Collections

Editorial collections should feel like magazine features.

Examples

Rainy Day Soundtracks

Tokyo Basement Gems

Jazz After Midnight

First Stop in Tokyo

Quiet Stores Worth the Walk

Women of City Pop

Sunday Morning Listening

Film Scores You'll Never Forget

Each title should invite curiosity.

Never use clickbait.

# Empty States

Empty states should feel encouraging.

Good

Your next favourite store is still waiting.

Good

Nothing saved yet.

Every collection starts with one record.

Good

Maybe tomorrow's crate has your name on it.

Avoid

No Data.

Nothing Here.

Empty.

---

# Notifications (Future)

Notifications should feel like gentle suggestions.

Good

A store you might like has new arrivals.

Good

Someone with your taste found something interesting.

Avoid

Don't miss this deal!

Limited stock!

---

# Error Messages

Be calm.

Good

Something went wrong.

Let's try again.

Good

We couldn't load this store right now.

Avoid

Fatal Error.

Unexpected Exception.

---

# Writing About Music

Never describe music using marketing language.

Prefer observations.

Good

Often visited by soundtrack collectors.

Good

Known for thoughtful jazz selections.

Good

A favourite among City Pop enthusiasts.

Avoid

The best soundtrack collection.

The biggest jazz inventory.

---

# Image Captions

Captions should feel documentary.

Good

Face Records, Tokyo

Good

A rainy afternoon in Shimokitazawa

Good

Fresh arrivals this Thursday

Avoid

Amazing Record Store

Best Vinyl Shop

---

# Editorial Rule

Every screen should feel like turning the page of a beautifully printed travel guide.

Not like browsing an online marketplace.

When uncertain,

always choose

calm over excitement,

curiosity over promotion,

stories over specifications.
