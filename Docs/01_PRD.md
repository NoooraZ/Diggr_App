# Diggr — Product Requirement Document

Version: MVP v1

---

# Product Overview

Diggr is a mobile-first vinyl discovery app designed for collectors, treasure hunters, and curious beginners.

Rather than helping users buy records online, Diggr helps them discover record stores that match their music taste.

The experience focuses on the joy of crate digging—finding unexpected records, exploring local record stores, and collecting memorable discoveries while traveling or exploring one's own city.

Diggr is not an e-commerce platform.

It is a discovery platform.

---

# Problem

Today, vinyl collectors discover record stores through fragmented sources:

- Reddit
- Xiaohongshu (RED)
- Instagram
- Google Maps
- Discogs
- Friends' recommendations

This workflow creates several problems:

- Store information is scattered across platforms.
- Many recommendations are outdated.
- Users cannot tell whether a store matches their music taste before visiting.
- Google Maps shows location, but not personality.
- Review platforms rarely describe what collectors actually care about.

Collectors often spend hours researching before deciding which stores are worth visiting.

---

# Vision

Help every vinyl collector answer one simple question:

> **"Which store is most likely to have something I'll love?"**

Diggr recommends stores based on taste rather than popularity.

---

# Target Users

Primary

### Collector

Experienced collectors who know exactly what they are looking for.

Examples:

- First Press collectors
- Jazz collectors
- Soundtrack collectors

---

### Treasure Hunter

Collectors who enjoy exploring unfamiliar stores.

They value discovery over efficiency.

They enjoy finding unexpected records.

---

### Vinyl Newbie

Users beginning their vinyl journey.

They may not know every artist, but they know what kind of music they enjoy.

Diggr helps them discover stores with confidence.

---

# Product Philosophy

Diggr celebrates:

- Discovery
- Personality
- Community
- Travel
- Local culture

Diggr does NOT prioritize:

- Online shopping
- Inventory management
- Price comparison

Inventory changes constantly.

Store personality lasts much longer.

---

# Core Value Proposition

Diggr helps users:

- Discover stores matching their taste
- Understand each store before visiting
- Listen to album previews (when available)
- Save stores around the world
- Learn from collectors with similar taste

---

# Core Loop

User opens Diggr.

↓

Discovers stores matching their taste.

↓

Checks Recent Finds and Store DNA.

↓

Visits the store.

↓

Finds a record.

↓

Logs the discovery.

↓

Returns to discover more stores.

Local collectors use Diggr to discover nearby stores.

Travellers build collections of stores they want to visit around the world.

# Core Features

## Treasure Map

A stylized treasure map instead of a traditional navigation map.

Users can:

- Explore cities
- Switch between current location and other cities
- Tap vinyl pins to preview stores

The map should encourage exploration rather than navigation.

---

## Store DNA

Every store has a unique personality.

Initially generated by AI.

Over time, refined through Treasure Logs and community activity.

Examples:

- City Pop
- Jazz
- Soundtracks
- Hidden Gem
- Collector Friendly
- Vintage

Store DNA describes personality rather than inventory.

---

## Recent Finds

Generated from users' Treasure Logs.

Similar to Letterboxd's "Popular Reviews This Week."

Purpose:

Help users understand what kinds of discoveries are happening inside a store.

Recent Finds are community-driven.

---

## New Arrivals

Published by store owners.

Similar to Instagram Stories.

Not a complete inventory.

Only a lightweight preview of recent arrivals.

Purpose:

Encourage users to visit stores in person.

---

## People Like You

Recommendations generated from collectors with similar taste.

Recommendations are based on:

- Taste overlap
- Store DNA
- Treasure Logs
- Community behaviour

Popularity alone should never determine recommendations.

---

## Album Preview

Album pages may include a short audio preview if online resources are available.

Purpose:

Help users quickly recognise unfamiliar records.

Album preview supports discovery.

It is not intended as a music streaming feature.

---

## Treasure Log

Represents a successful discovery.

Each Treasure Log includes:

- Album
- Store
- Condition
- Price
- Short caption
- Date

Treasure Logs are memories rather than reviews.
